Biography

I am currently a Ph.D. candidate at Ming Hsieh Department of Electrical Engineering, University of Southern California, under the guidance of Prof. Bhaskar Krishnamachari. My research is focused on enabling the Industrial Internet of Things (IoT). We are developing algorithms to optimize reliability, delay and energy efficiency of networks based on Timeslotted Channel Hopping (TSCH) protocol.

I am part of Autonomous Networks Research Group, one of the world leading academic groups in the research and development of low-power wireless technologies. I have been involved in projects that cover multiple aspects of IoT, from hardware design, networking, sensing and data processing.

I received my M.Sc. in Computer Science (2011) and B.Sc. in Computer Engineering (2007) degrees from the University of Campinas, Brazil. My advisor was Prof. Nelson L. S. da Fonseca. My Master’s thesis made contributions in the area of resource management and optimization of hybrid Radio-over-Fiber (RoF) networks. We studied the problem of physical constraints on the delay and throughput of RoF architectures with long fiber lengths and proposed algorithms for the Base Stations placement problem.

I have also worked in industry for 4 years in research and development of wireless technologies, including projects with ZigBee networks, RFID, Real Time Localization based on Wi-Fi. I have lead projects involving firmware and software for wireless modules based on TI CC430, CC1101 and other radio technologies.
